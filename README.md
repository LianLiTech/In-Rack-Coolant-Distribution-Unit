The In-Rack Coolant Distribution Unit (CDU) is an advanced liquid cooling solution designed for high-density server racks and containerized data centers. By delivering precise thermal management directly at the rack level, In-Rack CDUs optimize cooling efficiency, reduce energy consumption, and support next-generation computing workloads.

Containerized data centers—compact, modular IT facilities built within standard shipping containers—are increasingly leveraging In-Rack CDUs to meet growing performance demands. These systems are ideal for edge deployments, rapid data center expansion, and environments with limited space or infrastructure.

Key Advantages

High Efficiency Cooling

Direct liquid-to-liquid cooling minimizes thermal resistance and hotspots.

Reduces the reliance on traditional air-based cooling, cutting overall PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness).

Scalable and Modular Design

In-Rack CDUs integrate seamlessly into containerized data centers, allowing plug-and-play expansion.

Supports high-density racks without the need for extensive facility modifications.

Energy Savings

Liquid cooling transfers heat more efficiently than air, lowering operational costs.

Enables use of free-cooling and warm-water cooling, further reducing power consumption.

Reduced Footprint

Eliminates the need for large CRAC units and extensive airflow management.

Ideal for containerized deployments in urban, edge, or space-constrained environments.

Enhanced Reliability

Integrated monitoring and control features prevent overheating and hardware throttling.

Reduces risk of downtime by maintaining consistent temperature across high-density racks.

Applications

In-Rack CDUs are well-suited for a range of modern data center environments:

Containerized Data Centers
Compact modular facilities benefit from the reduced cooling footprint and high efficiency of In-Rack CDUs.

High-Performance Computing (HPC)
Handles intense workloads like AI training, scientific simulations, and financial modeling.

Edge Data Centers
Delivers reliable cooling in remote or urban edge deployments with minimal infrastructure.

Telecommunications and 5G Infrastructure
Supports high-density networking and compute nodes in distributed networks.

Future Prospects

The integration of In-Rack CDUs in containerized data centers is set to expand as the demand for sustainable, high-density, and edge-ready IT infrastructure grows:

AI and Machine Learning Workloads: Increased server density requires precise, efficient cooling solutions.

Sustainable Data Centers: Liquid cooling reduces energy consumption and carbon footprint, aligning with global sustainability goals.

Edge Expansion: Modular CDUs enable rapid deployment of containerized data centers closer to end-users.

Intelligent Monitoring: Future CDUs will integrate AI-driven thermal management and predictive maintenance for smarter operations.

With these trends, In-Rack CDUs are positioned to become a standard in next-generation data center architecture, providing a balance of efficiency, scalability, and reliability.
